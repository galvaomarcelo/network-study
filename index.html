Study title: Schematic network evaluation

Dear Participant, thank you for your participation in the study.

Researcher(s):

Marcelo Galv√£o galvao.marcelo@uni-muenster.de (PhD candidate of Geoinformatics)

Purpose of the study:

We investigate how people use maps and orient themselves while driving. Part of the problem is to understand how different kinds of maps benefit the user. In this study, we evaluate different map simplification methods in the context of route reading and orientation.

Procedure:

In this study, you will evaluate six maps. For each map, there is two simplified versions, and you need to compare them and give your answer to five simple questions. For some questions, you just need to answer if you prefer one map over the other objectively. For others, you need to recognize a marked feature in a map and mark the same feature in another map by drawing with the mouse. It is required a computer or a laptop connected to the internet i.e., no tablet or smartphones. Please send your responses (including the figures) to the responsible researcher e-mail at the end of the experiment.

Duration:

10-15 minutes

Potential risks:

The study offers no risk for the participant.

Privacy:

Original data obtained from this study will be anonymised and only processed in aggregate. In such form, it might be published in academic journals, presentations or other media, but never in a way that would allow individual identification. One week after the completion of the study it might no longer be possible to retract your data from such aggregated analyses.

Benefits:

While you may not benefit from this research personally, the data collected in this user study will benefit the research community in better understanding how people use maps to orient themselves.

You are free to stop, quit the study and retract your data at any time during the study with no further consequences.

If you have any questions, please ask them now.

For further questions, complains or issues, please contact the institute's Ethics-Committee: ifgi.ethics-app@uni-muenster.de.
